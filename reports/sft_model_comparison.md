## Summary observations

Contrary to the intended effect, instruction fine-tuning made most answers shorter, more casual, and *less* reliable rather than more domain-accurate: 9 of 10 SFT answers were judged worse than the untrained base model's. The SFT model picked up the terse, opinionated, sometimes tangential "Reddit answer" register of the underlying `finance-alpaca`/FiQA source data rather than a clear, structured FAQ-answering register, and it introduced its own fabricated numbers in places (Q4's "$45k"). The one case where SFT won (Q7, compound interest) was because the base model's answer contained fabricated pseudo-math while SFT's was a plain, correct explanation with no invented numbers. This suggests the SFT stage would benefit from a larger, more curated instruction dataset (more direct FAQ-style answers, fewer forum-opinion answers) and/or more training steps to actually improve on the base model rather than trade structure for tone.
